What it is

Eudaimonia

yoo-dai-mon-EE-a  ·  Ancient Greek

Aristotle's word for flourishing — not a fleeting high, but the lifelong practice of living well.

He meant it as a verb — flourishing is something you do, daily, by acting like who you mean to be. Eudaimon starts with its smallest, hardest rep: going to bed when you said you would.

Why I built it

I'm David — a 21-year-old student and hardtech founder. And someone who's fought doomscrolling for years.

It sucks. A lot. I've tried every solution out there — the screen-blocking apps, all of it — and nothing held for long. So I spent the last months with the smartest people I know, drafting something that finally works.

So here's what we built.

A bedside box that takes your phone at night, holds you to your own wind-down, and gives it back in the morning.

Eudaimon — the structure you build before you need it

Why it works

The you at 11pm isn't the you who set the alarm at 8am. Same body, different will.

Willpower runs on your prefrontal cortex, and by night it's spent — so most apps fail, asking your most depleted self to make the call. Eudaimon flips it: morning-you makes the rules, and the box holds them when evening-you would break them. And 80% of getting better is just getting to bed on time.

  • A sheepdog, not a cop — it catches you, never shames you, and stays quiet when you're fine.
  • Relief, not discipline — the calm of having already decided, hours ago, when it was easy.
  • Optional accountability — a partner gets a quiet note if you miss bedtime. Never your apps, usage, or location.

The science behind it → Eudaimon principles

The night, walked through

While you're on track, it's just soft guidance — it only makes itself felt the moment you start to slip.

01

Wind-down

One gentle ping: ease off now. Not a buzzer — a soft chime you can wave away with a tap.

02

The evening routine

A short checklist you wrote yourself — each item negotiable or non-negotiable, so a hard night still ends in bed.

03

The phone goes in the box

Your phone goes in a real box that physically detects it. No tap or checkbox can fake the commitment.

04

If you stall, it shifts tone

It doesn't just get louder — gentle, then firm, then your own words on the screen. No "comply" button; you finish by doing the thing.

05

Morning gives it back

Light before the alarm, then a small task to prove you're up — no snooze, no math. Then the phone is yours again.
